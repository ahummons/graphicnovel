Ability to identify and analyze the techniques and components in comics and graphic novels, their potential meaning and significance to visual storytelling Ability to connect various forms of visual storytelling to cultural manifestations in other disciplines in the liberal arts Ability to identify the subject matter of comics and graphic novels, their potential meaning and significance, and the larger intellectual, historical, or political trends that impact their production. Demonstrate a general knowledge of the history of comics and graphic novels from its beginning to the present including major historical events and current trends. Understand the key concepts and debates underlying theories of comics and graphic novels. Demonstrate critical understanding of the technological and creative processes involved in the writing and production of comics and graphic novels Understand the basics of storytelling craft in the construction of comics and graphic novels. Effectively research, develop, present, critically evaluate and give peer feedback on creative and scholarly work.
